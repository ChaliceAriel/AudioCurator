About AudioCurator

An open source Audio Blog

A passion for music


The main purpose for AudioCurator was to create an easy-to-implement template for anyone who would like to curate, comment on, and post their own selection of music to share with their personal audiences.

We have created a well-documented READ.ME in our GitHub repo, and have thoroughly commented our code to allow anyone to incorporate this template as a standalone blog, or link to it in their personal portfolios.

The creators

Brian

Brian's previous skills in computer networking, troubleshooting, and server administration/virtualization have continued to be very useful in web development and he is currently interested in continuing to learn more.

He has been an avid underground music fan for years, and loves discovering new and unheard tracks that are good for the brain. AudioCurator was a natural project to combine his love for music and developing coding skills.

Chalice

Chalice's coding adventure started when a friend invited her to join his tech startup as a User Experience Designer in 2014. In January of 2015, her senior year at MSU Bozeman (BA in Music Technology), She took a Computing for The Arts course. She learned to write programs for Arduino microcontrollers and built a 64-input, lighted midi controller and an MP3 player.

She has been continuing her education in programming and code and is excited to begin working on new projects. Besides code, she enjoys incorporating her musical past in the present by singing Kirtan in a group setting as well as for yoga classes. She is also a rather talented cello player.

Jeslyn

Jeslyn's interest in code began earlier this year, as a couple different members of her family began their own coding adventures and gave her insight into the code and tech community as whole. She loves the idea of building and creating new things that didn't previously exist, as well as learning new languages. She has a deep-seated love of music having studied voice for 8 years and is an amateur guitar player herself.

Jeslyn has had a nomadic upbringing with roots in the Pacific Northwest and Bozeman, MT. She has recently settled in Helena, MT and enjoys the benefits of living anywhere where outdoor pursuits are aplenty.
